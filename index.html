Hi there, I'm Kaushik Manikonda!

I am an interdisciplinary machine learning engineer with a background in Industrial engineering, Data Science, Statistics, Business Analytics, Astrophysics, and Petroleum engineering. I am a joint doctoral student in the Harold Vance Department of petroleum engineering, and a master's student in the Wm Michael Barnes '64 Department of Industrial & Systems Engineering at Texas A&M University, College Station. I received a bachelor’s degree in Astrophysics, and Mechanical engineering from Arizona State University in 2017, before moving on to receive a master’s degree in petroleum engineering from Texas A&M University. I worked on the National Aeronautics and Space Administration’s (NASA) HASP project between 2016-2017, before starting my master’s program at Texas A&M University. My current research areas include, Machine learning applications for drilling automation, Leveraging data science techniques for early gas kick detection, and Big data analytics in the oil & gas industry. I am currently spending a few semesters abroad, at Texas A&M University - Qatar in Doha, Qatar as a visiting machine learning researcher. During my time at TAMUQ, I will be leading the TAMUQ Flow Assurance Student Club as the president.
